<div class="research-intro">

  <div class="research-text">
    <p class="lead">
      Our laboratory focuses on understanding the fundamental mechanisms that govern tissue regeneration and developing next-generation regenerative therapies based on stem cells and organoid technologies.
    </p>

    <p>
      We aim to bridge basic stem cell biology with translational applications, with a particular emphasis on restoring tissue function in degenerative and chronic diseases.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="research-image">
    <img src="/images/research_main.jpg">
  </div>

</div>

<div class="research-body">

<p>
A central theme of our research is to uncover how regenerative capacity is regulated at the cellular and molecular levels, and how it can be enhanced or reprogrammed.
</p>

<p>
Among these, aging represents one of the major biological contexts in which regenerative function is compromised.
</p>

<p>
By investigating the interplay between stem cell function, tissue environment, and disease states, we seek to identify strategies that can restore or even enhance regeneration.
</p>

</div>

## Research Areas

<div class="research-grid">

  <div class="research-card">
    <img src="/images/research1.jpg">
    <h3>Regulation of Stem Cell Function and Tissue Regeneration</h3>
    <p>
      We investigate the key molecular mechanisms that regulate stem cell function and tissue regeneration using intestinal stem cells as a model system.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="research-card">
    <img src="/images/research2.jpg">
    <h3>Organoid-Based Disease Modeling and Drug Screening</h3>
    <p>
      We develop advanced organoid models to recapitulate disease states and establish drug screening pipelines.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="research-card">
    <img src="/images/research3.jpg">
    <h3>Organoid-Based Regenerative Therapy</h3>
    <p>
      We translate organoid technologies into therapeutic strategies and evaluate their efficacy in disease models.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>
